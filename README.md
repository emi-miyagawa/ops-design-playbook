## Case Studies

### 1. Rule Design for Exception Handling

**Problem**  
Strict validation rules blocked exception cases, causing frequent rejections and operational delays.

**Constraint**  
- Limited authority to change core policies  
- Need to maintain compliance and auditability  

**Approach**  
- Identified that most rejections were not violations, but acceptable exceptions  
- Replaced hard validation errors with warning-based control  
- Allowed exceptions while preserving visibility  

**Decision**  
Prioritized operational continuity over strict enforcement.

**Trade-off**  
- Risk: Potential misuse  
- Mitigation: Maintain visibility and monitoring instead of blocking  

**Result**  
- Rejection rate reduced by ~50%  
- Stabilized operations at scale

**Why this matters**
- Enables scalable operations under constraints
- Reduces dependency on individual judgment

### 2. Data Granularity Redesign

**Problem**  
Data tracked at a high-level (customer-based), making status tracking and automation difficult.

**Constraint**  
- No full system redesign authority  
- Existing data needed to remain usable  

**Approach**  
- Redefined unit of management (customer → asset-level)  
- Introduced tracking history  
- Adjusted key structure for traceability  

**Decision**  
Changed granularity instead of adding manual tracking layers.

**Trade-off**  
- Increased data complexity  
- Improved visibility and automation capability  

**Result**  
- Eliminated manual verification  
- Enabled state tracking and scalability  

---

### 3. Choosing Manual Control over Automation

**Problem**  
Frequent exceptions made full automation unreliable.

**Constraint**  
- High variability in cases  
- Automation would increase failure risk  

**Approach**  
- Evaluated automation vs structured manual handling  
- Implemented checklist-based control instead of automation  

**Decision**  
Selected controlled manual process over unstable automation.

**Trade-off**  
- Less efficiency than full automation  
- Higher reliability and flexibility  

**Result**  
- Reduced errors  
- Achieved stable and repeatable operations  

---

### 4. Upstream Root Cause Fix Across Functions

**Problem**  
Recurring data errors required repeated corrections in downstream processes.

**Constraint**  
- Issue originated outside of direct control  
- Cross-functional coordination required  

**Approach**  
- Traced error source upstream  
- Identified root cause in data input process  
- Requested correction and added detection mechanism  

**Decision**  
Addressed root cause instead of continuing downstream fixes.

**Trade-off**  
- Required coordination effort  
- Eliminated recurring workload  

**Result**  
- Prevented future errors  
- Reduced operational friction

## Design Principles

- Prioritize operability over perfect control  
- Design for non-standard cases, not just happy paths  
- Reduce cognitive load for users  
- Separate processing layers for stability  
- Translate ambiguity into decision logic  
- Avoid over-automation when variability is high  
- Enable systems that non-experts can operate  
- Solve problems at the source, not downstream
